Introduction

Asia takes upto 30% as the of the world'd land area and sitting as the world's largest continent. Encompasing 48 countries and multiple climate zones, it is not without a reason why the continent is consisting of diversified animal species. However, as humans rapidly expand their territories into the primitive wilderness, along with increasing demand for food, climate change, Asia may possibly be the continent where the most species embracing extinction due to human activities. Until today, many Asian countries remain to be developing countries, which means in order to boost the growth they will continue relying on their unsustainable economic models and exploit land resources. In other words, it is a substantial stride further driving animals to extinction. Below are some of Asia's critically endangered species, the threats they face and the steps we can take to protect life on land for future generations.

Threats to Life on Land

Life on land faces five main threats. These are deforestation, climate change, habitat loss, pollution and the spread of invasive species. These interconnected threats severely impact ecosystems and species on land.

Endangered Species in Asia

Some of the critically endangered species in Asia are Sumatran Orangutan, Javan Rhino, Asian Elephant and Philippine Eagle. These species have experienced major population losses in recent years.

What can we do to help?

We need to address each of the threats to land and the way that we live. To loss of habitats, people urgently need to stop destroying natural areas, especially rainforests. To combat climate changes, people need to adapt the way they live and work to become more planet friendly. With regards to overuse, wildlife trade needs to be watched so that people don't get carried away destroying and selling nature to make money. To get a handle on pollution, littering and pesticide pollution needs to stop.
