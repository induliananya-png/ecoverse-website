Unique Land Animals Around the World

Explore some of the most fascinating and rare land animals that inhabit our planet.

Axolotl - Mexico

The Axolotl is a highly endangered salamander species found in Mexican lakes and canals. They are also known as Mexican walking fish. Axolotl's are famous for their ability to regenerate limbs.

Okapi - Democratic Republic of Congo

The Okapi is a rare species native to Central African rainforest. They are commonly know as forest giraffe. These animals are biologically related to the giraffe. They have a appearance similar to a horse but stripes like zebras.

Saiga Antelope - Central Asia

The Saiga is known for it's unique snout which points downwards. This snout helps the animals to regulate temperature and filtering the dust. Saiga is one of the oldest and most endangered animals in the world. They can be found in Central Asia

Quokka - Australia

Quokka is a small marsupial that live in Rotten Island. They are also known as "world's happiest animal" because of their friendly smile. Quokka is herbivorous and nocturnal.

Pangolin - Africa & Asia

Pangolins are the only mammals that has protective keratin scales. They mainly feed on termites and ants and is nocturnal. Because of their high market value for scales and meat these animals are frequently traded.

Gerenuk - East Africa

Gerenuks are a antelope species that is known for their elegance appearance and their ability to reach tree leaves with their long necks and standing on their hind legs.

Maned Wolf - South America

Despite the name "Maned Wolf" they are not real wolves. These creatures are mostly found in South American grasslands. It has a characteristic black mane around it's neck and reddish fur. Maned wolf is distinguished by its "fur-on-sitting" appearance.
